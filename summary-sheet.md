Summary Sheet

1. What does this HRMS exist to deliver, and to whom?

This HRMS exists to maintain accurate employee records and salary information. The primary beneficiaries are HR administrators and employees who depend on correct workforce and payroll data.

2. What is the single most dangerous bug pattern you found across your testing?

The most dangerous bug pattern is data inconsistency caused by database or API failures. If employee information is not stored or updated correctly, it can affect multiple operations throughout the system.

3. Which test in your suite are you most proud of, and why?

The employee creation test is the most important because it verifies that new employee records are successfully stored and displayed. It prevents failures where employee data is lost during onboarding.

4. What did you choose NOT to automate, and why was that the right call?

I chose not to automate UI appearance and responsive design testing. These areas require human judgment and visual verification, making manual testing more practical.

5. What's one thing in your submission that you're not fully confident about, and why you included it anyway?

I am not fully confident about advanced edge-case coverage because of time limitations. I included the tests and documentation to demonstrate my testing approach and understanding of quality practices.

6. What changed between your first approach and your final submission?

Initially, I focused only on building application functionality. Later, I added testing considerations, quality documentation, and risk-based thinking to improve the overall reliability of the project.

7. What do you not know about quality engineering or construction payroll that you would need to learn before doing this job?

I would need to learn:

- Construction payroll regulations and compliance requirements.
- Overtime calculation policies.
- Advanced test automation and CI/CD best practices for production environments.

8. If you had one more day, what would you test that you didn't get to?

I would perform security testing, performance testing, and larger-scale database testing. These areas are important because they protect employee data and ensure the system remains reliable as usage grows.
